Eba & Ewedu

Description

Eba & Ewedu is a nigerian delicacy popular among the Yorubas of south west Nigeria. Eba is a white or yellow mould prepared from "garri", while Ewedu is a vegetable "draw" soup. The combo of this duo is ... heavenly

Ingredients

  • Cassava flakes or "garri"
  • Ewedu
  • Beef
  • Fish
  • Palm Oil
  • Tomato
  • Bell pepper or "rodo"
  • Potash or "kan un"
  • Salt
  • Seasoning

Steps to Prepare Eba

  1. Put water on fire.
  2. Allow the water to boil properly.
  3. Get the cassava flakes or "garri" ready
  4. Pour the boiled water into a big bowl or pot
  5. Immediately you pour the "garri" into the hot water
  6. Then stir with the stirring rod
  7. Continue stirring till the mixture is fresh to the eyes and smooth to touch

Steps to prepare Ewedu

  1. Blend the bell pepper and tomato to make a fine paste
  2. Fry both fish and meat (this is entirel your choice)
  3. Blend the ewedu also
  4. Put a water on fire
  5. add a very small quantity of potash into the water
  6. Then add the blended ewedu
  7. Add salt and seasoning to the soup on fire
  8. The soup tends to foam or rise from the pot, so ocassional stirring is required till the ewedu is soft
  9. Soft ewedu is a sign that it's edible already
  10. Put another pot on fire
  11. Add the blended pepper
  12. Allow the pepper to cook well
  13. Then add palm oil
  14. Let the palm oil steam with the pepper
  15. Add salt, seasoning and other spices
  16. Add the fish and meat
  17. Let it all steam together
  18. Best served hot
